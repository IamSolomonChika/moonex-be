## Liquidity Provision

### Becoming a Liquidity Provider

#### What is Liquidity Provision?

When you provide liquidity, you're essentially:
- **Adding funds** to a trading pair
- **Earning fees** from trades
- **Receiving LP tokens** representing your share
- **Contributing** to market efficiency

#### Getting Started

1. **Choose a Pool**
   - Navigate to "Liquidity" section
   - Browse available pools or create a new one
   - Check historical performance and fees

2. **Add Liquidity**
   - Select the amount you want to provide
   - For dual pools, provide both tokens
   - For single-sided pools, provide one token (platform adds the other)

3. **Review Details**
   - **Expected Returns**: Estimated APR/APY
   - **Fee Structure**: Trading fees you'll earn
   - **Impermanent Loss Risk**: Potential value changes
   - **Gas Costs**: Transaction fees

4. **Provide Liquidity**
   - Approve token usage in your wallet
   - Confirm the transaction
   - Receive LP tokens in your wallet

#### Managing Liquidity

**Adding More Liquidity**
- Add to your existing position
- Increase your LP token holdings
- Boost your earnings proportionally

**Removing Liquidity**
- Withdraw your provided funds
- Burn corresponding LP tokens
- Receive original tokens plus fees earned

**Impermanent Loss Explained**
- Occurs when token prices diverge from initial deposit
- Risk is higher for volatile token pairs
- Mitigated by earning trading fees over time

### Pool Types

#### Standard Pools
- **Two-sided liquidity**: Provide both tokens
- **50/50 ratios**: Balanced token amounts
- **Lower risk**: More stable returns

#### Single-Sided Pools
- **One token provided**: Platform adds the other
- **Convenience**: No need to balance tokens
- **Higher fees**: Convenience has a cost

#### Concentrated Liquidity (V3)
- **Custom price ranges**: Target specific price points
- **Capital efficiency**: Better returns for specific ranges
- **Active management**: Requires monitoring and adjustment

## Yield Farming

### Understanding Yield Farming

Yield farming involves staking your crypto assets to earn rewards, typically in the form of additional tokens. Think of it as earning interest on your crypto holdings.

#### Farm Types

1. **Standard Farms**
- Stake LP tokens from liquidity pools
- Earn platform tokens as rewards
- Variable APR based on pool performance
- Moderate risk, good returns

2. **Auto-Compound Farms**
- Automatically reinvest earnings
- Higher APY through compounding
- Set-and-forget convenience
- Higher fees for auto-compounding service

3. **Locked Farms**
- Lock funds for a specific period
- Higher rewards for longer commitments
- Reduced liquidity during lock period
- Higher risk, better returns

#### Getting Started with Farming

1. **Browse Available Farms**
   - Navigate to "Yield Farming" section
   - Filter by protocol, asset, or APR
   - Review farm details and performance

2. **Select a Farm**
   - Check the farm's:
     - **APR/APY**: Annual returns
     - **TVL**: Total value locked (indicates stability)
     - **Lock Period**: Any commitment requirements
     - **Risk Assessment**: Farm security rating

3. **Stake Your Assets**
   - Choose the amount to stake
   - Review staking details and fees
   - Approve token usage in your wallet
   - Confirm the transaction

4. **Monitor Your Position**
   - Track earnings in real-time
   - See harvest schedules
   - Manage multiple positions
   - Compound when optimal

### Yield Farming Strategies

#### Diversification
- **Multiple Farms**: Spread risk across different pools
- **Different Protocols**: Mix PancakeSwap, Apeswap, etc.
- **Various Assets**: Include stablecoins and major tokens

#### Rebalancing
- **Regular Portfolio Review**: Monthly or quarterly
- **Harvest and Reinvest**: Compound earnings
- **Adjust Allocations**: Based on performance and goals
- **Tax Considerations**: Track for tax reporting

#### Risk Management
- **Start Small**: Test with small amounts first
- **Research Thoroughly**: Understand each farm's mechanics
- **Monitor Regularly**: Stay updated on farm performance
- **Set Limits**: Decide when to take profits or cut losses

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: Is the platform safe to use?**
A: Yes, we implement industry-leading security measures including multi-sig wallets, encryption, and comprehensive security audits.

**Q: What networks are supported?**
A: We currently support BSC (Binance Smart Chain) mainnet and testnet.

**Q: How are gas fees calculated?**
- **Base Fees**: Network transaction costs
- **Complexity Fees**: Based on transaction complexity
- **MEV Protection**: Additional fees for privacy protection
- **Dynamic Pricing**: Varies with network congestion

### Trading Questions

**Q: What is slippage tolerance?**
A: The maximum price change you'll accept. Higher tolerance = higher execution chance but worse price.

**Q: Can I cancel a transaction?**
A: Transactions can be cancelled if they haven't been confirmed by the network yet.

**Q: What happens if I lose my private key?**
A: Unfortunately, lost private keys cannot be recovered. Always keep them secure and backed up.

### Liquidity Questions

**Q: What is impermanent loss?**
A: Value loss when token prices diverge from when you provided liquidity. Mitigated by earning fees over time.

**Q: When should I remove liquidity?**
A: Consider removing when you need the funds, when impermanent loss becomes significant, or when rebalancing.

**Q: How are fees calculated?**
- **0.25% Standard**: Most PancakeSwap pools
- **Variable Fees**: V3 pools use dynamic fee tiers
- **Protocol Fees**: Additional platform fees may apply

### Yield Farming Questions

**Q: What's the difference between APR and APY?**
- **APR**: Annual Percentage Rate (simple interest)
- **APY**: Annual Percentage Yield (includes compounding)

**Q: When do I get rewards?**
- **Standard Farms**: Harvest manually anytime
- **Auto-Compound**: Automatically reinvested at optimal times
- **Locked Farms**: Only available after lock period expires

**Q: Is yield farming risky?**
- **Smart Contract Risk**: Platform could have vulnerabilities
- **Market Risk**: Token values can decrease
- **Impermanent Loss**: Could lose value vs. holding tokens
- **Liquidity Risk**: May not be able to withdraw immediately

### Technical Questions

**Q: Why is my transaction stuck?**
- **Gas Issues**: Insufficient gas limit
- **Network Congestion**: High network activity
- **Nonce Issues**: Incorrect nonce sequence

**Q: How do I check transaction status?**
- **Transaction Hash**: Use block explorers like BscScan
- **Platform Dashboard**: View in transaction history
- **Wallet App**: Check transaction status in your wallet

**Q: What are RPC endpoints?**
- **Network Nodes**: Servers that connect to BSC blockchain
- **Free Options**: Several public endpoints available
- **Private Options**: Paid services for better reliability
